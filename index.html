Li Ma

Professor

The University of Chicago Department of Statistics and Data Science Institute

I develop and investigate statistical and machine learning methods for analyzing complex data. Data can be complex due to a variety of reasons: the measurement characteristics (e.g., high-dimensionality, multi-modality, noise structure), the underlying latent structures, and the experimental or sampling schemes. Modern applications often involve a combination of all these sources. My research areas span generative models, multi-scale models and methods, nonparametric inference, Bayesian inference and computation. A current focus is on statistical assessment and diagnostics of generative models and AI algorithms.

I am a faculty member in the Department of Statistics and the Data Science Institute at the University of Chicago.
